## SECTION 1 — EXECUTIVE VERDICT

**What Recall Touch should actually be:** The Revenue Execution OS — an AI-powered system that ensures no business opportunity dies from poor follow-up, missed communication, or human delay. Not a generic AI receptionist. Not a chatbot. Not a CRM. It is the execution layer that sits between your existing tools and your revenue — making sure every inbound lead gets answered, every follow-up happens on time, every no-show gets recovered, every dead lead gets resurrected, and every quote gets chased.

**What it should NOT be:** A general-purpose AI assistant, a full CRM replacement, a marketing automation tool, or a cheap answering service. It should never compete on "we're the cheapest AI calls" — that is a race to zero margin.

**Where it can realistically win:** The massive, underserved space between "CRM that logs data but doesn't act" and "human staff that costs $4,000/month and still misses calls." Local service businesses (dental, HVAC, legal, med spas, roofing, real estate) feel this pain most acutely. They lose $5K-$50K/month in missed calls, forgotten follow-ups, and no-show appointments. They cannot afford full-time staff for every gap, and existing tools (Calendly, HubSpot, generic AI bots) don't execute — they just organize.

**Strongest path to 9 figures:** Vertical-by-vertical domination of local service businesses ($297-$2,400/mo ARPU), expanding into multi-location and franchise operations ($5K-$25K/mo), then white-labeling to agencies/resellers (thousands of sub-accounts). The wedge is "answer every call, recover every missed opportunity" for single-location service businesses. The expansion is "become the revenue execution backbone for every service business in North America."

**Highest-probability wedge:** Single-location home services and healthcare businesses (dental, med spa, HVAC, plumbing, legal intake). They have urgent pain (missed calls = lost revenue NOW), budget ($297-$497/mo is trivial vs one recovered job), fast onboarding (phone number + business hours + greeting = live in 10 minutes), and clear ROI proof (X calls answered, Y appointments booked, Z revenue recovered).

**Highest-upside wedge:** Multi-location franchise operations and the agency/reseller channel. A single franchise deal = 50-500 locations × $500/mo = $300K-$3M ARR from one contract. The agency channel creates distribution leverage — 500 agencies each managing 20 accounts = 10,000 paying accounts without direct sales cost.

**Standalone vs acquisition:** Build for standalone scaling with acquisition optionality. The most attractive acquirers (ServiceTitan, Podium, Housecall Pro, Mindbody, Toast, or even Twilio/Vonage) want to buy what they can't easily build: a working AI execution layer with proven vertical expertise, embedded customer workflows, and real revenue data. Building standalone to $20-50M ARR makes the company attractive at 10-15x revenue for a strategic acquisition ($200M-$750M+).

## SECTION 8 — PRODUCT ARCHITECTURE

### 6.1 Universal Omnichannel Inbox
- Why: Single view of all customer conversations across voice, SMS, email, webchat
- MVP: Voice call logs + SMS threads, unified timeline per contact
- Advanced: Full omnichannel with routing rules, team assignment, SLA tracking, sentiment analysis
- Complexity: Medium (event-driven architecture, channel abstraction layer)
- Monetization: Core feature, included in all plans. Premium: team routing, SLA alerts
- Moat: Conversation history creates switching cost. Every month of data makes the platform harder to leave.

### 6.2 Contact/Entity Memory Graph
- Why: AI must remember past interactions, preferences, history, and context across all touchpoints
- MVP: Contact record with call history, notes, tags, basic timeline
- Advanced: Relationship graph (who referred whom, family members at same practice, connected entities), AI-generated contact summaries, predicted next-best-action
- Complexity: Medium-High (vector storage for semantic memory, graph relationships)
- Monetization: Premium AI memory features on higher tiers
- Moat: Deep. The more conversations processed, the better the AI understands each contact. Impossible to replicate by switching tools.

### 6.3 Event Timeline
- Why: Every interaction (call, SMS, email, booking, no-show, payment) on a single timeline per contact
- MVP: Chronological list of events with basic metadata
- Advanced: AI-summarized timeline, "what happened since last contact" briefing, milestone markers
- Complexity: Low-Medium
- Monetization: Core feature
- Moat: Low standalone, high when combined with memory graph

## SECTION 9 — MODE SYSTEM DESIGN

Recall Touch's power comes from serving multiple user archetypes with a single codebase. One application, three distinct modes, each with its own onboarding flow, dashboard layout, and default templates. The mode system is the product's secret to broad market appeal without becoming a confused mess.

### Core Architecture: One Codebase, Mode Selection During Onboarding

During first login, users select their primary use case:
- **Solo Mode** — Personal follow-up, not business operations
- **Sales Mode** — Pipeline execution and deal management
- **Business Mode** — Inbound capture, booking, recovery, ROI (default for service businesses)

Mode selection loads:
1. Industry-specific templates (scripts, workflows, sequences)
2. Role-specific dashboard layouts
3. Relevant feature emphasization
4. Terminology and language in UI
5. Recommended integrations and settings

This is not three separate products. It's one product with intelligent defaults that adapt to context.

### Solo Mode

**Target User:** Freelancers, consultants, coaches, creators, individual professionals

**Primary Jobs:**
- Personal opportunity follow-up (callbacks, networking follow-ups)
- Invoice/payment chasing
- Callback handling for personal service clients

- Networking follow-up automation
- Personal appointment/booking management
- Life admin (dentist reminders, car maintenance, etc.)

**Emotional Job:** "I never drop the ball. I honor every relationship, opportunity, and commitment."

## SECTION 19 — RETENTION, STICKINESS, AND EXPANSION

Retention is the single highest-leverage metric for a SaaS business. Recall Touch must build operational embeddedness and habit loops from day 1.

### Habit Loops & Engagement Mechanics

**Daily Habit: Dashboard Check-In**
- Users log in to see "What happened last night while I was sleeping?"
- Hero metric: Revenue recovered overnight
- Secondary: Calls answered, appointments booked, follow-ups executed
- Friction: <30 seconds from login to hero metric visible
- Retention impact: Users who check dashboard 3+ times/week have >90% retention vs 40% for inactive users

**Weekly Habit: Digest Email**
- Every Monday: "Here's what Recall Touch did for you last week"
- Content: calls answered (count + estimated revenue), appointments booked, follow-ups sent, no-shows recovered
- Gamification: "Streak: 27 days of consistent follow-up execution"
- Conversion mechanic: "Your follow-ups converted 3 leads this week. Upgrade to Scale to get advanced analytics."
- Retention impact: Digest recipients have 20-25% higher retention vs non-recipients

**Monthly Habit: ROI Report**
- Executive summary: "Revenue Impact Report for [Month]"
- Metrics: total revenue recovered, calls answered, show rate vs industry benchmark
- Comparison: "You're 40% better than average [industry] practice for response time"
- CTA: upgrade opportunity when hitting usage limits
- Retention impact: Customers who review monthly ROI reports have 85%+ 12-month retention

**Operational Embeddedness: Phone Number Lock-In**
- Business prints phone number on trucks, business cards, Google listings, websites
- Switching Recall Touch means reprinting everything (cost: $1K-$5K)
- Changing phone number loses SEO equity built over months
- This is underrated: 30-50% of retention is simply "too expensive to switch"
- Moat strength: High, immediate

**Operational Embeddedness: AI Agent Customization**
- After 2-4 weeks, users customize: greeting scripts, FAQ rules, industry-specific knowledge base
- Agent becomes fine-tuned to their business
- Switching means rebuilding all customization in new system
- This is critical lock-in: takes 20-40 hours to fully train an AI agent

**Operational Embeddedness: Workflow & Follow-Up Sequences**
- Users build custom follow-up sequences: missed call → SMS → email → call
- Sequences optimized over weeks/months
- Switching means rebuilding every workflow
- Lock-in cost: 30-50 hours of configuration + learning curve

**Habit: Staff Training**
- Team members trained on Recall Touch
- Receptionist uses dashboard daily
- Sales team integrates into pipeline
- Staff turnover would require retraining competitor's platform
- Switching friction: >50 hours of team retraining

### Switching Costs Quantified

**Monetary Switching Costs:**
- Phone number change: $500-$2,000 (domain transfer, SEO impact, reprinting materials)
- Time to reconfigure in new system: 40-80 hours × $50-100/hour labor = $2,000-$8,000
- Lost productivity during migration: 1-2 weeks of reduced call handling = $1,000-$5,000 in missed revenue
- **Total switching cost: $3,500-$15,000**

At blended ARPU of $668/mo, this equals 5-22 months of subscription value. Extremely high switching cost.

**Emotional Switching Costs:**
- Risk: "What if the new system doesn't work as well?"
- Trust: Already familiar with how Recall Touch works
- Fear: New system = new training, new problems

### At-Risk Signals & Churn Prevention

**Early Warning Signals (track weekly):**

1. **Engagement Drop** — Customers not logging in regularly
   - Threshold: 7+ days without login (low risk), 14+ days without login (high risk)
   - Action: Trigger "We miss you" email with "Here's what happened while you were away" summary

2. **Usage Drop** — Using <20% of allocated minutes
   - Threshold: 2+ weeks of <20% utilization
   - Action: "It seems you're not using all your minutes. Downgrade to save money?" OR "Want help optimizing your setup?"

3. **Billing Complaints** — Support tickets about pricing or overages
   - Threshold: Any complaint about surprise charges or unclear billing
   - Action: Immediate response, explanation, potential credits for goodwill

4. **Failed Calls / Technical Issues** — Uptick in failed call handling
   - Threshold: >2% call failure rate, multiple dropped calls
   - Action: Technical diagnosis, priority support, potential credit if our fault

5. **Inactive Follow-Up Sequences** — No new campaigns or sequences created
   - Threshold: 4+ weeks with 0 sequence creation
   - Action: "Let's review your follow-up strategy. Want a free consultation?" (NRR recovery)

6. **Low Booking Rate** — Calls answered but few appointments booked
   - Threshold: <15% of calls result in bookings (below benchmark)
   - Action: "Your call-to-booking rate is below industry average. Let's diagnose." (Product help)

**Churn Prevention Playbook:**

When at-risk signals trigger:
- **Engagement drop** — Send curated digest focusing on wins from their data
- **Usage drop** — Offer downgrade option (transparent) OR identify if setup is wrong
- **Billing complaints** — Human response within 1 hour, explain clearly, offer credit
- **Technical issues** — Escalate to engineering, offer priority support, credit for downtime
- **Inactive sequences** — Offer free strategy call with CS team, provide template sequences
- **Low booking rate** — Share benchmark data, offer AI script optimization

**Outcome:** These interventions should prevent 40-50% of at-risk churn.

## SECTION 21 — TRUST, RISK, AND COMPLIANCE

### Risk: AI Hallucination / Misinformation
- Danger: AI tells a caller the wrong business hours, quotes a wrong price, makes a false promise
- Mitigation: Strict grounding — AI only uses information explicitly provided in the knowledge base. If uncertain, it says "Let me have someone get back to you on that." Configurable "never say" rules. Call recording review.
- Product safeguard: "Guardrails" settings page where businesses define what AI can/cannot say. Post-call AI audit flagging potential issues.

### Risk: Wrong Follow-Up Timing
- Danger: Sending a follow-up at 2am, following up too aggressively, following up on a cancelled request
- Mitigation: Business hours enforcement on all outbound. Contact preference tracking. Unsubscribe/opt-out handling. Frequency caps.

### Risk: Bad Automation Behavior
- Danger: Infinite loop of follow-ups, sending to wrong contact, duplicate messages
- Mitigation: De-duplication logic, maximum message limits per contact per period, human review queue for edge cases, "pause all automation" emergency button.

### Risk: Spam/Telecom Compliance
- Danger: Getting phone numbers flagged as spam, violating TCPA, A2P messaging regulations
- Mitigation: Opt-in tracking, proper A2P 10DLC registration, call frequency limits, "STOP" handling for SMS, caller ID reputation monitoring, compliance documentation.

### Risk: Privacy & Data Security
- Danger: Call recordings containing sensitive information, HIPAA violations for healthcare, GDPR for EU
- Mitigation: Encryption at rest and in transit, role-based access, data retention policies, HIPAA BAA for healthcare tier, call recording consent announcements, data export/deletion on request.

### Risk: Bad AI Voice Experience
- Danger: AI sounds robotic, has latency, misunderstands the caller, creates frustrating experience
- Mitigation: Invest in top-tier voice synthesis (ElevenLabs Conversational), tune latency aggressively (<800ms response time target), implement graceful fallback ("I didn't catch that, could you repeat?"), offer call transfer to human as escape hatch.

### Risk: Low Trust Onboarding
- Danger: Business owners don't trust AI to talk to their customers
- Mitigation: Test call during onboarding (hear the AI yourself), call recording access from day 1, "review mode" where AI answers but sends summary to owner before taking action, gradual trust-building (start with after-hours only, expand to all calls).

### Trust-Building UX Elements
- Transparent AI: "Your AI said this on the call" with full transcript
- Control: Easy override of any AI action, "teach the AI" feedback mechanism
- Proof: "Here's what would have happened without Recall Touch" counterfactual
- Safety: One-click pause all AI activity, instant human takeover

## SECTION 22 — WHAT TO CUT / WHAT NOT TO BUILD

### CUT or DELAY:

**1. Full CRM** — Do not build a CRM. Integrate with existing ones. Building a CRM is a 5-year, $50M+ project and you will lose to HubSpot, Salesforce, and ServiceTitan. Stay in the execution layer.

**2. Email marketing platform** — Do not build Mailchimp. Use email as a channel within follow-up sequences, but don't build campaign design tools, template builders, or email analytics dashboards.

**3. Social media management** — Completely out of scope. Do not touch this.

**4. Website builder / landing pages** — Not your business. Refer to partners.

**5. Generic chatbot for websites** — Commoditized, low value, distracts from voice/phone which is the hero capability.

**6. Video calling / telehealth** — Different product category entirely. Don't get distracted.

**7. Inventory / POS systems** — Vertical-specific infrastructure. Not your problem.

**8. Accounting / invoicing (full)** — Don't build QuickBooks. Invoice CHASING (follow-up on overdue invoices) yes. Invoice CREATION no.

**9. Solo user as primary target** — Build it eventually but never prioritize over business users. The economics don't work as a primary segment.

**10. Free tier** — Never. Free trial yes. Free ongoing no. Free users consume support, create noise, and never convert at rates that justify the cost.

**11. AI image generation, document creation, or general AI assistant features** — Stay focused. You are a revenue execution system, not a general AI tool.

- Event-driven architecture: triggers fire on events (missed_call, new_lead, no_show, quote_sent, appointment_created, contact_dormant)

- Enrollment manager: tracks contact position in sequences, handles deduplication, opt-outs, max frequency

- Calendar sync: Google Calendar + Outlook bi-directional sync via OAuth
- Availability calculator: merge business hours + calendar events + buffer time → available slots
- Booking handler: create appointment, sync to calendar, send confirmation SMS, schedule reminders
- Reminder chain: 24h → 2h → 30min before appointment, configurable per workspace

### Event Processing
- Real-time event bus for: call started, call ended, SMS received, appointment created, lead captured, follow-up due
- Webhook delivery system for CRM sync and external integrations
- Event replay capability for debugging
- Dead letter queue for failed deliveries

### Conversation Memory
- Per-contact memory store: key facts, preferences, history summary (stored as structured JSON + optional vector embedding)
- AI-generated contact summaries after each interaction
- Memory retrieval during live calls (feed context to AI agent)

### Lead Scoring / Priority Layer
- Rule-based initial scoring: recency, engagement level, deal value, source quality

- Priority queue for follow-up: highest-scored leads get followed up first

### Analytics Aggregation
- Daily rollup job: compute daily_metrics from raw events

- Real-time counters for dashboard: calls today, appointments today, using Redis/in-memory cache

### Billing Meter Events

### Observability
- Structured logging for all API routes and background jobs
- Error tracking with context (workspace_id, user_id, action)

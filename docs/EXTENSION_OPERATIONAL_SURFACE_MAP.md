# EXTENSION — OPERATIONAL SURFACE MAP

**Source extension for where behavior manifests. Living contract: [SYSTEM_DOCTRINE.md](./SYSTEM_DOCTRINE.md).**

---

The doctrine defines behavior.  
This document defines where behavior manifests in real businesses.

The system must not attempt to "support all operations".
It must attach to universal operational breakpoints — the places where money, commitments, and responsibility most commonly fail.

Each surface must produce canonical signals and map into the operational chain.

The goal is not feature coverage.
The goal is economic leverage coverage.

---

# CORE SURFACES (PHASE 1 — REQUIRED FOR INSTALLATION)

These surfaces exist in nearly every business and generate immediate dependency.

## 1. Incoming Work

**Sources:** messages, forms, calls, bookings, requests  

**Failure prevented:** unanswered opportunity  

**Chain responsibility:** Signal → Decision → Authority → Action  

**Dependency effect:** businesses stop manually checking inboxes  

---

## 2. Commitments

**Sources:** scheduled calls, appointments, promised follow-ups, agreed tasks  

**Failure prevented:** silent drop  

**Chain responsibility:** Decision → Delivery → Closure → Proof  

**Dependency effect:** calendar becomes reliable reality  

---

## 3. Payments

**Sources:** invoices, failed payments, deposits, subscriptions  

**Failure prevented:** recoverable money loss  

**Chain responsibility:** State → Decision → Action → Reconciliation  

**Dependency effect:** finance stops requiring supervision  

---

## 4. Responsibility Transfer

**Sources:** staff handoffs, assignment, ownership change  

**Failure prevented:** orphaned work  

**Chain responsibility:** Authority → Action → Integrity  

**Dependency effect:** managers stop coordinating manually  

---

## 5. Outcomes

**Sources:** completion, cancellation, no-show, failure  

**Failure prevented:** uncertain reality  

**Chain responsibility:** Closure → Integrity → Proof  

**Dependency effect:** business knows what actually happened  

---

# EXPANSION SURFACES (PHASE 2 — RELIANCE)

**Retention** — prevents customers silently leaving  

**Reactivation** — recovers dormant value  

**Upsell timing** — expands value when conditions allow  

**Fulfillment monitoring** — ensures promised work actually finishes  

---

# NETWORK SURFACES (PHASE 3 — PROPAGATION)

- Client ↔ Provider shared commitments  
- Partner coordination  
- Contractor execution verification  
- Multi-party delivery records  

These create cross-organization dependency.

---

# IMPLEMENTATION RULE

Every new capability must attach to an operational surface.

Never build abstract features.

Instead:
**Identify failure surface → Map signals → Enforce outcome → Provide proof.**

---

# PRIORITY ORDER

1) stabilize internal business  
2) stabilize customer relationship  
3) stabilize financial relationship  
4) stabilize multi-party relationship  

Scaling occurs only after step 4.

---

# SUCCESS CONDITION

When all phase-1 surfaces are installed:

**A business can no longer operate normally without the system.**

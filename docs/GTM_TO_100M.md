# GTM to $100M

**Revenue Operator — Commercial Execution Infrastructure**

Go-to-market and expansion strategy. Institutional tone.

---

## Vertical assault order

1. **Real estate** — Motivated seller acquisition, appointment setting, compliance (fair housing, disclosures). High transaction value; governed execution reduces liability.
2. **Insurance** — Underwriting lead intake, consent and disclosures, jurisdiction-specific rules. Regulatory density favors deterministic, auditable execution.
3. **Solar** — Qualification pipelines, consent capture, disclosure requirements. Clear compliance and commitment capture.
4. **Legal** — Intake and compliance gating, conflict checks, jurisdiction lock. Natural fit for approval modes and audit trail.
5. **Financial** — Suitability screening, disclosures, record retention. Execution-intent and template-only model align with regulatory expectations.
6. **Healthcare** — Intake with consent and compliance; no diagnosis or treatment. HIPAA-safe positioning; consent and disclosure enforcement.
7. **B2B / High-ticket** — Appointment setting, multi-party confirmation, contract and renewal. Enterprise features (dual approval, jurisdiction lock) support deal size.
8. **Debt negotiation / Retention / Multi-party** — Structured flows, disclosure confirmation, cross-party confirmation. Domain packs and compliance pack are central.

Order prioritizes regulatory intensity and willingness to pay for governance and auditability.

---

## Enterprise lighthouse strategy

- Land **regulated vertical** logos (insurance, legal, financial) with jurisdiction lock, dual approval, and audit export. Use as references for compliance and governance narrative.
- Position as **infrastructure**, not tool: “Execution continues without renewal interruption”; “Infrastructure is scoped per organization.” Contract terms and SLA hooks support enterprise sales motion.
- Case studies and references emphasize: **governed execution**, **replayable audit**, **no freeform AI**, **compliance enforced**. No charts or KPIs in positioning; focus on certainty and continuity.

---

## Solo credibility flywheel

- Solo ($297/mo) and Growth ($897/mo) prove category and reduce perceived risk for larger deals. Prebuilt industry presets and minimal configuration support “live in 60 seconds” without exposing internal complexity.
- Dashboard language reinforces infrastructure: governed strategy applied, operational execution active, commitments secured, compliance enforced, execution anchored. No “performance improvement” or SaaS KPIs; confidence reinforcement and confirmation summaries only.
- Solo success stories and category education (whitepapers, positioning) feed enterprise pipeline. “Solo first, then Team/Enterprise” is a credible path.

## Industry-specific landing pages

- One landing or positioning slice per vertical: real estate, insurance, solar, legal, financial, healthcare, B2B, retention/recovery. Each page stresses: **governed execution**, **compliance**, **jurisdiction**, **commitment confirmation** — not channels, packs, or seats. Copy aligns with category lock (no chatbot, automation, workflow builder, campaign tool, sequence engine, CRM replacement).

## Sales motion for enterprise

- Lead with **compliance and audit**: template-only messaging, append-only audit trail, jurisdiction lock, dual approval. Demo approval modes, compliance pack, and audit export.
- **Contract reference enforcement** and **SLA monitoring hooks** (internal readiness, no analytics UI) support procurement and security reviews. Enterprise feature overrides via JSON config allow contract-level customization without product drift.
- Multi-location and cross-region regulatory matrices support distributed organizations. Role-based approval control and compliance officer override address governance requirements.

---

## Annual plan conversion strategy

- Positioning: “Annual commitment preferred for operational continuity. Two months at no charge. Execution continues without renewal interruption.” Emphasize **continuity** and **reliability**, not discount. Feature gating remains deterministic; no SaaS-style limits (channels, packs, seats) in copy.

---

## Compliance-driven upsell

- Move from autopilot → preview_required → approval_required → jurisdiction_locked as compliance needs increase. Dual approval and compliance officer override are Team/Enterprise features. Upsell narrative: “More control and auditability without changing how execution works.”

---

## Governance-driven expansion

- Expand within account via **multi-location**, **new jurisdictions**, **new domain packs** (e.g. legal, financial, healthcare). Each pack adds strategy graph, objection tree, disclosures, escalation; same infrastructure, more coverage. Governance and approval features support larger and more regulated teams.

---

## Expansion via multi-location

- Enterprise and Team support **multi-location isolation**. Franchise and distributed orgs can run governed execution per location with central audit and policy. Cross-region regulatory matrices and jurisdiction lock support expansion without new product categories.

---

## Integration ecosystem expansion

- Connector ingest (webhooks, CRM, CSV, calendar, missed call, SMS reconciliation, email threading, transcript, manual entry) is append-only and idempotent. More connectors increase addressable workflows without changing core execution model. Execution remains intent-based; integrations feed the same pipeline.

---

## Category education whitepaper

- Publish and maintain a **category whitepaper**: Commercial Execution Infrastructure vs. chatbot, automation, workflow builder, campaign tool, sequence engine. Emphasize: governed execution, compliance-aware execution, jurisdiction-locked execution, deterministic execution, commitment-confirming infrastructure, operational continuity layer. Use in demand gen and enterprise conversations.

## Regulatory positioning advantage

- In regulated industries, “we never send freeform AI; every message is from an approved template and compliance pack” is a differentiator. Replayable audit trail, jurisdiction lock, and dual approval support RFP and security questionnaires. Regulatory defensibility is a GTM asset, not only a risk control.

## 1. BEST CATEGORY TO OWN

### Category Options Evaluated

**Option A: "AI Revenue Execution System"**
- Current positioning. Sounds impressive. Nobody searches for it. Nobody knows what it means. A plumber who needs calls answered won't type this into Google. An agency owner won't tell their client "we use a revenue execution system." The words are too abstract, too consultant-brained. It impresses founders, not buyers.
- **Clarity:** 3/10. **Distinctiveness:** 6/10. **Buyer recognition:** 1/10. **Rejected.**

**Option B: "AI Receptionist" / "AI Phone Answering"**
- This is where the market searches. It's the real category. But it's terminally commoditized — 30+ products, race to the bottom on price, feature parity everywhere. Owning "AI receptionist" means competing on price against $29/month tools. Recall Touch cannot win this fight at $297/month.
- **Clarity:** 9/10. **Distinctiveness:** 1/10. **Premium feel:** 2/10. **Rejected as primary category.**

**Option C: "AI Follow-Up Platform"**
- Accurate to the differentiated value. But "follow-up" sounds small, tactical, low-stakes. It doesn't justify $297+/month. "Follow-up" also means different things to different people — could be email sequences, could be CRM reminders, could be SMS. Too ambiguous.
- **Clarity:** 6/10. **Distinctiveness:** 5/10. **Premium feel:** 3/10. **Rejected.**

**Option D: "Revenue Recovery Platform"**
- Strong ROI framing. "Revenue recovery" is a real concept that resonates with business owners — they know they're losing money. But it's narrow. It implies the business is already broken and this is a remediation tool, not a growth tool. And "revenue recovery" in SaaS usually means dunning/failed payments, which creates category confusion with Stripe billing recovery tools.
- **Clarity:** 7/10. **Distinctiveness:** 6/10. **Premium feel:** 5/10. **Close but not right.**

**Option E: "AI Revenue Operations" / "RevOps AI"**
- RevOps is a real, growing category but it's owned by enterprise tools (Clari, Gong, 6sense). Using "RevOps" invites unfavorable comparisons and signals a different buyer persona (VP Revenue, not plumbing company owner).
- **Clarity:** 5/10 (for target market). **Distinctiveness:** 4/10. **Premium feel:** 7/10. **Wrong audience.**

**Option F: "The AI That Closes the Loop"**
- This is a tagline, not a category. But the concept it represents — closing the loop on every call, every lead, every follow-up, every no-show, every quote — is the actual product thesis. The problem with every business is not that the phone doesn't ring. It's that nobody finishes the job after the phone rings. The call gets answered but the follow-up doesn't happen. The appointment gets booked but nobody confirms. The quote gets sent but nobody chases it. The patient no-shows and nobody re-books. Every step in the revenue process has a gap, and those gaps are where money dies.
- This isn't a Google-searchable category. But it's the strategic position.

### FINAL CATEGORY DECISION

**Primary category claim:** "AI Revenue Closer"

**What it means:** Recall Touch is the AI that closes every open loop in your business — the missed call, the unsent follow-up, the unbooked appointment, the no-show, the unchased quote, the cold lead, the forgotten callback. Other tools answer your phone. Recall Touch finishes the job.

**Why this works:**
- "Revenue" signals money, ROI, commercial value. Business owners pay for revenue.
- "Closer" signals completion, execution, finishing. It implies action, not just reception.
- It is NOT the same as "AI receptionist" (passive, answering). It's active — it closes things.
- It's broad enough to cover: call answering, follow-up, booking, reminders, recovery, reactivation, quote chasing, no-show recovery.
- It justifies premium pricing. An AI receptionist is worth $29-79/month. An AI that closes revenue loops is worth $297+/month.
- It naturally expands: solo users close personal loops (invoices, callbacks, outreach). Sales teams close deal loops. Businesses close operational loops.

**SEO/search strategy alongside the category:** The product pages and landing pages still target "AI answering service," "AI receptionist," "missed call recovery," "appointment booking AI" for search traffic. But the brand positioning, homepage, and conversion messaging lead with "AI Revenue Closer" to differentiate and justify price.

**One-line category definition for all contexts:**

"Recall Touch is the AI that answers, follows up, books, reminds, recovers, and closes — so no opportunity falls through."

## 3. BEST FOCUSED-ENTRY WEDGE

The broad vision serves solo users through enterprise. But you cannot launch to everyone simultaneously. The entry wedge must be the segment where:

- Pain is acute and quantifiable
- Ability to pay $297+/month is trivial relative to the value
- Speed to value is fast (days, not months)
- Support burden is low
- The customer can become a case study
- The segment is reachable through targeted outbound
- Retention is structural (the AI becomes part of their operation)

### The Wedge: Single-Location Service Businesses With Inbound Call Volume

**Specifically:**
- Dental practices
- Med spas / aesthetics clinics
- HVAC / plumbing / electrical
- Legal intake (personal injury, family law, immigration)
- Roofing / restoration / home services
- Chiropractic / physical therapy
- Veterinary clinics

**Why this wedge specifically:**

1. **Quantifiable pain.** These businesses know exactly what a missed call costs. A plumber knows a missed call is a $300-800 lost job. A dentist knows a missed new patient is worth $2,000-5,000 in lifetime value. A personal injury firm knows a missed intake call is worth $5,000-50,000.

2. **$297/month is trivial.** If one recovered call per month covers the cost (and it does for all of these verticals), the ROI argument is unassailable.

3. **Call volume is predictable.** These businesses get 10-50 inbound calls per day. Enough to prove value fast, not so much that costs spiral.

4. **They already pay for answering services.** Many use Ruby, Smith.ai, AnswerConnect, or local answering services at $200-500/month. Recall Touch replaces something they already budget for, plus adds follow-up.

5. **They are reachable.** Every dental practice, law firm, and HVAC company has a Google Business Profile, a website, and a phone number. Outbound outreach is straightforward.

6. **Switching is easy.** Forward your existing number. Takes 2 minutes. No integration required for initial value.

7. **They can become case studies.** "Dental practice recovers 14 patients/month" or "HVAC company captures $4,200/month in previously missed revenue" are powerful, specific, believable proof points.

**What the wedge is NOT:**

- Not "service businesses" broadly — too vague
- Not agencies — too complex, too many stakeholders, longer sales cycle
- Not enterprise — too slow, too many requirements
- Not solo freelancers — pain isn't acute enough, can't pay $297/mo
- Not SaaS companies — wrong product for their needs
- Not "anyone with a phone" — not a segment

## 5. FINAL PRODUCT VISION

### What Recall Touch Is

Recall Touch is the AI that closes every revenue loop in your business.

It answers your calls. It follows up on your leads. It books your appointments. It confirms your schedule. It recovers your no-shows. It chases your quotes. It reactivates your cold prospects. It does this across voice, text, and email — automatically, 24/7, with the persistence and consistency that no human team can match.

### Who It Is For

Phase 1: Single-location service businesses that depend on inbound calls — dental, medical spa, legal, home services, healthcare.

Phase 2: Multi-location operators, agencies managing client accounts, real estate teams.

Phase 3: Sales teams, consultants, coaches, solo operators (via Solo Mode at lower price point).

### What Outcomes It Drives

1. **Missed revenue recovered.** Calls that went to voicemail now get answered. Leads that went cold get reactivated. No-shows get rebooked. Quotes get chased.
2. **Operational time freed.** The business owner or office manager spends zero time on follow-up logistics. The AI handles it.
3. **Revenue visibility created.** The business can see exactly how many leads were captured, how many appointments were booked, how many no-shows were recovered, and what dollar value was at stake.
4. **Consistency guaranteed.** Every call gets answered the same way. Every follow-up goes out on time. Every no-show gets a text. There is no human variance, no sick days, no turnover.

### Why It Is Hard to Replace

1. **The AI learns the business.** Over time, the AI accumulates knowledge about services, pricing, common questions, preferred scheduling, and customer history. Leaving means retraining from scratch.
2. **The phone number becomes embedded.** Once customers, Google Business Profile, and marketing materials point to the Recall Touch number, switching is operationally painful.
3. **The follow-up workflows become the operating system.** The no-show recovery flow, the reactivation campaign, the quote chasing sequence — these become how the business operates. Removing them means building manual processes from scratch.
4. **Revenue attribution creates dependency.** Once the owner sees "Recall Touch recovered $4,200 this month," canceling means accepting they'll lose that revenue.

### Why Buyers Will Pay Real Money

Because one recovered call pays for the entire month. A dental practice pays $297/month. One new patient is worth $2,000-5,000. If Recall Touch captures one patient who would have called and gotten voicemail, the ROI is 7-17x in month one. This math is simple, believable, and verifiable. No ROI argument in B2B SaaS is stronger than "the product costs less than the revenue from a single recovered opportunity."

### How It Spans Solo + Sales + Business Without Becoming Messy

Through modes — not through separate products. The core engine is the same: contact timeline, follow-up workflows, communication channels, analytics. The modes change:

- **What's visible** (Solo sees a simple dashboard, Business sees full operations, Sales sees pipeline)
- **What's pre-configured** (Solo gets personal follow-up templates, Business gets industry-specific workflows, Sales gets lead qualification and pipeline stages)
- **What language is used** (Solo uses "contacts" and "reminders," Business uses "leads" and "revenue," Sales uses "prospects" and "pipeline")
- **What metrics are shown** (Solo sees "tasks completed," Business sees "revenue recovered," Sales sees "meetings booked and pipeline value")

The database, the workflow engine, the voice layer, and the billing system are identical underneath. The modes are presentation layers, not separate products.

## 6. CORE PRODUCT ARCHITECTURE

### Module 1: Omnichannel Inbox

**Purpose:** Single view of every conversation with every contact across voice, SMS, and email.

**Why it matters:** This is the daily driver. If the inbox is good, the user opens the product every day. If it's bad or confusing, they check it once and forget about it.

**Must-have version (launch):**
- Threaded contact timeline: every call transcript, SMS, email in chronological order per contact
- Unread/flagged/action-needed filters
- Quick reply via SMS or email from within the timeline
- AI-generated summary of each contact's history ("Sarah called twice about a Thursday appointment, booked on second call, confirmed via text")
- Search across all conversations

**Advanced version (later):**
- Suggested replies
- Auto-categorization (new lead, existing customer, urgent, spam)
- Contact merging (same person, different phone/email)
- Team assignment and handoff within threads

**Retention value:** Very high. The inbox becomes the operational command center. Leaving means losing conversation history and the daily workflow.

**Monetization value:** Medium. It's included in all plans but the advanced features (team assignment, suggested replies) gate to higher tiers.

**Implementation complexity:** Medium-high. The hard part is the real-time sync between voice transcripts arriving asynchronously, SMS threads, and email — all unified per contact with no duplicates.

**Cost risk:** Low. Inbox is storage and compute, not per-minute voice/LLM costs.

### Module 2: AI Follow-Up Engine

**Purpose:** Automatically execute follow-up sequences across voice, SMS, and email based on triggers, timing, and contact behavior.

**Why it matters:** THIS IS THE PRODUCT. The follow-up engine is what separates Recall Touch from every AI receptionist. Answering calls is the hook. Follow-up execution is the product.

**Must-have version (launch):**
- Trigger-based follow-up: missed call → SMS in 60 seconds → voicemail follow-up in 2 hours → second SMS in 24 hours
- Appointment confirmation: booking → confirmation SMS → reminder 24 hours before → reminder 2 hours before
- No-show recovery: missed appointment → SMS in 30 min → call attempt next day → re-booking offer in 48 hours
- Quote follow-up: quote sent → check-in SMS in 24 hours → call in 3 days → final text in 7 days
- Basic timing and cadence controls
- Stop on reply / stop on booking / stop on opt-out

**Advanced version (later):**
- Multi-branch workflows (if reply = positive → book, if reply = negative → offer discount, if no reply → escalate)
- A/B testing of follow-up messages
- AI-written personalized follow-up based on call transcript
- Reactivation campaigns for contacts dormant 30/60/90 days
- Lead scoring integration (high-value leads get faster, more persistent follow-up)

**Retention value:** Extremely high. Once a business relies on automated follow-up, turning it off means leads fall through again. The pain of cancellation is immediate and measurable.

**Monetization value:** Very high. Follow-up volume is the natural upsell lever. Solo gets 3 active follow-ups. Business gets unlimited. This is the right feature to tier on.

**Implementation complexity:** High. The workflow engine needs to handle: timing (cron-like scheduling), triggers (event-driven), multi-channel execution (voice, SMS, email), state management (what step is this contact on), and stopping conditions. This is essentially a lightweight workflow automation engine.

**Cost risk:** Medium. Each follow-up action that uses voice or LLM incurs cost. SMS is cheap (~$0.01/segment). Voice follow-up calls are expensive (~$0.15-0.30/min). The engine needs cost controls per workflow.

### Module 3: Voice Layer (Inbound + Outbound)

**Purpose:** Answer inbound calls with natural AI voice. Make outbound calls for follow-up, reminders, and reactivation.

**Must-have version (launch):**
- Inbound call answering with configurable greeting, business knowledge, appointment booking capability
- Call transcription and summary
- Call recording with playback
- Outbound calls for: appointment reminders, no-show follow-up, lead follow-up
- 6 voice presets included, premium voices as add-on
- Configurable business hours / after-hours behavior
- Warm transfer to human when needed
- Max call duration configurable per agent

**Advanced version (later):**
- Custom voice cloning (business owner's voice)
- Multilingual calls
- Real-time sentiment detection
- Call coaching for human team members
- Voicemail drop for outbound

**Retention value:** Very high. Voice is the most tangible, most impressive feature. Hearing your AI handle a real call creates emotional investment.

**Monetization value:** Very high. Voice minutes are the primary billing unit. This is where revenue and cost both concentrate.

**Implementation complexity:** Already largely built. Vapi + ElevenLabs + Deepgram stack is functional.

**Cost risk:** HIGH. Every minute of voice conversation costs $0.12-0.17 in COGS across the provider stack. This is the margin-critical module. Must be metered, must have overage pricing, must have controls.

### Module 4: Booking Engine

**Purpose:** Book, confirm, reschedule, and manage appointments directly from calls, texts, and follow-up workflows.

**Must-have version (launch):**
- Google Calendar and Outlook sync (bi-directional)
- AI books during calls based on real-time calendar availability
- Confirmation SMS after booking
- Reschedule via text reply
- Calendar view in dashboard
- Booking link for manual sharing

**Advanced version (later):**
- Multi-provider scheduling (book with specific team member)
- Waitlist management
- Buffer time configuration
- Group booking
- Integration with industry-specific booking systems (Dentrix, Mindbody, Clio)

**Retention value:** High. Once the calendar is synced and the AI is booking, removing it means going back to manual scheduling.

**Monetization value:** Medium. Included in all plans (critical decision — see below). The advanced features (multi-provider, industry integrations) gate to higher tiers.

**Implementation complexity:** Medium. Calendar API integration is well-documented. The hard part is conflict handling (double-bookings, timezone issues, real-time availability during a live call).

**Cost risk:** Low. Calendar API calls are negligible cost.

### Module 5: Analytics & Revenue Attribution

**Purpose:** Show the business owner exactly what Recall Touch did and what it was worth.

**Why it matters:** This is the retention engine. If the owner sees "$4,200 in pipeline from Recall Touch this month" every time they log in, they will never cancel. If they see vague activity metrics, they'll question the value.

**Must-have version (launch):**
- Calls answered (total, by hour, by day)
- Leads captured (with source: inbound call, missed call recovery, reactivation)
- Appointments booked (with status: confirmed, completed, no-show, cancelled)
- Follow-ups executed (SMS sent, calls made, emails sent)
- Estimated revenue impact: leads × average job value (configurable by the business)
- Simple dashboard card: "This month: 47 calls answered, 12 leads captured, 8 appointments booked, estimated value: $6,400"

**Advanced version (later):**
- Revenue confirmed vs. estimated (integration with payment/POS)
- Trend charts (week over week, month over month)
- Benchmarks vs. similar businesses
- Team performance (which agent/number performs best)
- Cost per lead, cost per appointment

**Retention value:** CRITICAL. This module must make the business owner feel like canceling Recall Touch means losing money. The revenue attribution card is the single most important UI element in the entire product.

**Monetization value:** Medium. Basic analytics in all plans. Advanced benchmarks and team analytics in Scale+.

**Implementation complexity:** Medium. The hard part is revenue attribution — estimating the dollar value of a lead requires knowing the average job/patient/case value, which the business must configure during onboarding.

**Cost risk:** Very low. Computation on stored data.

### Module 6: Contact & Lead Management

**Purpose:** Maintain a record of every person who interacts with the business through Recall Touch.

**Must-have version (launch):**
- Auto-created contact on first interaction (call or text)
- Contact timeline (all interactions chronologically)
- Lead status: new → contacted → qualified → booked → completed / lost
- Basic tags and notes
- Export contacts

**Advanced version (later):**
- AI lead scoring
- CRM sync (HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, Zoho)
- Custom fields
- Segment lists for campaigns
- Duplicate detection and merging

**Retention value:** High over time. Contact data accumulates and becomes valuable. Leaving means losing or exporting the relationship history.

**Monetization value:** Medium. Basic CRM in all plans. Native CRM sync in Scale+.

**Implementation complexity:** Medium. The contact model exists. The timeline aggregation across channels is the harder piece.

**Cost risk:** Low. Storage.

### Module 7: Team & Admin

**Purpose:** Multi-user access, roles, permissions, and multi-location management.

**Must-have version (launch):**
- Invite team members by email
- Roles: owner, admin, member (view-only)
- Activity log (who did what)

**Advanced version (later):**
- Multi-location hierarchy (one account, multiple businesses)
- Per-location analytics and settings
- White-label for agencies
- SSO for enterprise

**Retention value:** High for teams. Once multiple people are using the system, switching requires organizational change.

**Monetization value:** High. Seat limits on lower tiers, unlimited on Scale+. Multi-location is enterprise pricing.

**Implementation complexity:** Medium. Workspace/role system exists in the codebase.

**Cost risk:** Low.

### What Is NOT a Core Module (Cut or Delay)

**Cut from launch:**
- Intelligence engines (40 directories) — over-engineered, no customer has validated
- Governance system — enterprise feature, not launch-critical
- Compliance tracking system — needed later, not for first 100 customers
- Delivery assurance system — internal operational tooling, not customer-facing value
- Continuity infrastructure — premature for a product without PMF
- Human safety constraints — important but can be simple rules, not a system
- Revenue lifecycle state machine — the actual revenue tracking is simpler than this implies

**Delay to post-PMF:**
- Pipeline view (Sales Mode feature, not needed for service business wedge)
- Template marketplace
- API access
- Advanced workflow branching
- Multi-language support (i18n is built but not needed for US service businesses)
- White-label

## 7. SOLO MODE DESIGN

### Target User

Individuals who need follow-through on personal or micro-business tasks: freelancers, consultants, coaches, solo practitioners, job seekers, creators.

### Emotional Job

"I have too many things to follow up on and they keep falling through the cracks. I feel disorganized and I know I'm leaving money on the table."

### Practical Job

- Follow up on unpaid invoices
- Follow up on unanswered outreach (pitches, applications, proposals)
- Remind myself about callbacks and commitments
- Keep professional relationships warm
- Never forget to follow up with a lead or prospect

### Core Workflows

1. **Add a follow-up.** "Follow up with Sarah about the proposal in 3 days." The system sends an SMS or email on the scheduled date. If no reply, it follows up again according to the cadence.
2. **Missed call capture.** If someone calls and the user is busy, the AI answers, takes a message, and the user gets a text summary. The AI can also offer to schedule a callback.
3. **Invoice chasing.** "Chase this invoice." The system sends a payment reminder SMS/email, follows up if unpaid after X days.
4. **Outreach follow-up.** "Follow up on my email to [contact] in 5 days if they don't reply." System monitors for reply (email integration) and sends follow-up if no response.
5. **Appointment management.** Book, confirm, remind — same engine as Business Mode but framed as personal schedule management.

### Why People Pay

Because one recovered invoice, one saved client relationship, or one recaptured opportunity per month justifies the cost. At $49/month (Solo tier from codebase), the bar is very low.

### Why They Stay

- The follow-up history becomes their professional memory
- The AI knows their contacts and context
- They've trained the system on their communication style
- Leaving means going back to manual follow-up (which they know doesn't work)

### Why They Refer

"I use this thing that makes sure I never forget to follow up. It literally chases my invoices for me." This is a simple, compelling word-of-mouth pitch.

### What to Avoid

- Do not make Solo Mode feel like a stripped-down business tool. It needs its own personality — lighter, more personal, less corporate.
- Do not show "leads" or "revenue recovered" language. Use "contacts," "follow-ups completed," "replies received."
- Do not include campaign features. Solo users don't run campaigns. They follow up on individual things.
- Do not gate appointment booking from Solo. It's core value even at the lowest tier.

### Pricing

$49/month (monthly) / $39/month (annual). 100 calls/month. 1 AI phone agent. 10 active follow-ups (not 3 — 3 is too restrictive to prove value). Basic analytics.

This tier exists to: build habit, create word-of-mouth, serve as a wedge into the personal productivity market, and upsell to Business when the solo operator's business grows.

## 8. SALES MODE DESIGN

### Target User

Sales reps, setters, closers, sales managers, high-ticket coaches, agencies running appointment-setting operations.

### Core Problem

Speed-to-lead, follow-up persistence, no-show recovery, and dead lead resurrection. Sales teams know that 50%+ of leads are never contacted, that response time is the #1 predictor of conversion, and that most reps give up after 1-2 attempts.

### Core Workflows

**Setter workflows:**
1. New lead comes in (from ad, form, referral) → AI calls within 60 seconds → qualifies (budget, timeline, authority, need) → books appointment with closer
2. Appointment confirmation → reminder sequence → no-show recovery
3. Lead not reached → multi-touch follow-up sequence (call, text, email over 7 days)

**Closer workflows:**
1. AI prepares pre-call brief from lead's previous interactions, qualification data, and any notes
2. Post-call: if not closed → AI executes follow-up sequence (proposal follow-up, objection-specific messaging)
3. If ghosted → AI runs "ghost recovery" sequence with escalating urgency

**Manager workflows:**
1. Dashboard: speed-to-lead (average time from lead to first contact), contact rate, booking rate, show rate, close rate
2. Rep comparison: which reps are following up, which are letting leads die
3. Pipeline view: leads by stage, velocity, bottlenecks
4. Revenue attribution: which lead sources produce closed revenue

### Lead Prioritization

AI scores leads based on: source quality, engagement signals (responded to text, answered call, asked about pricing), timing (how recent), and fit (qualification data). High-scored leads surface at the top. This prevents reps from cherry-picking and ensures the best leads get the fastest response.

### No-Show Recovery

This is a killer feature for sales teams. When a prospect no-shows:
1. AI sends "we missed you" text within 15 minutes
2. AI calls to rebook within 2 hours
3. If no response, AI sends a different angle text in 24 hours ("I know you're busy, here's a link to rebook when it works for you")
4. After 3 attempts, lead moves to "cool-off" and gets reactivated in 7 days

### Dead Lead Resurrection

Leads that went cold 30-90 days ago get a reactivation sequence:
- "Hey [name], we spoke back in [month] about [topic]. Are you still looking for help with that?"
- If positive reply → route to sales rep
- If no reply → try once more in 14 days, then archive

### Why It Retains

- Speed-to-lead metrics create accountability. Once the team sees "average response time: 47 seconds with AI vs. 6 hours without," they cannot go back.
- The no-show recovery and ghost recovery flows generate measurable saved deals. Each saved deal reinforces the value.
- Pipeline visibility becomes the management dashboard. Removing it blinds the manager.

### Why It Beats Generic Sales Tools

Outreach, Salesloft, Apollo, and similar tools are email-first. They sequence emails and sometimes calls but they do not make the calls. They do not actually talk to leads. They do not book meetings autonomously. Recall Touch in Sales Mode is the tool that does the work, not just the sequencing.

### Pricing for Sales Mode

This should be the Business tier ($297/month) or a Sales-specific variant at similar pricing. Sales teams generate enough revenue per booked meeting ($500-5,000+) that $297/month is trivial.

### DELAY SALES MODE TO PHASE 2 OF LAUNCH

Sales Mode should not be in v1. Here's why:
- The entry wedge is service businesses, not sales teams
- Sales teams have specific competitors (Outreach, Apollo, Aloware) with deep feature sets
- Building Sales Mode properly requires: pipeline view, lead scoring, rep management, speed-to-lead metrics, CRM integration
- Launching half-baked Sales Mode hurts credibility in a market that's already skeptical
- Service business mode and sales mode have different UX needs

**Launch plan:** Build and validate Business Mode first. Add Sales Mode as a distinct product surface 6-12 months post-launch, after the core follow-up engine is battle-tested with real service business customers.

## 9. BUSINESS MODE DESIGN

### This Is the Primary Mode at Launch

Everything in v1 should be optimized for a single-location service business that gets inbound calls and needs to convert those calls into booked appointments and revenue.

### Core User

Office manager or business owner at a dental practice, med spa, HVAC company, law firm, or similar. They are:
- Not technical
- Time-poor
- Responsible for making sure the business runs
- Measured on: appointments booked, revenue, patient/customer satisfaction
- Frustrated by: missed calls, unreliable front desk staff, follow-up falling through cracks

### Onboarding Flow (Revised)

The current 5-step flow is: Industry → Business Info → AI Agent Config → Teach AI → Go Live.

**Revised flow — 3 steps to first value:**

**Step 1: "Tell us about your business" (90 seconds)**
- Business name
- Industry (dropdown: dental, med spa, HVAC, plumbing, legal, roofing, other)
- Website URL (optional — if provided, scrape for business info)
- Primary phone number

**Step 2: "Connect your phone" (60 seconds)**
- Option A: Forward your existing number (instructions per carrier)
- Option B: Get a new number (instant provisioning)
- Option C: "I'll do this later, just show me the product" (skip to dashboard with demo data)

**Step 3: "You're live" (immediate)**
- AI agent is auto-configured with industry-specific defaults (greeting, knowledge base, appointment types, business hours)
- User sees their first dashboard with a prompt: "Call your number to test it" or "Listen to a sample call"
- Customization (agent name, greeting, business hours, services) happens AFTER first value, not before

**Why this is better:**
- Time to first "wow" goes from 15-20 minutes to 3-5 minutes
- No decision fatigue before seeing value
- Industry templates handle the configuration that users shouldn't have to think about
- Customization is a day-2 activity, not an onboarding gate

### Missed-Call Recovery Flow

1. Call comes in → AI answers, handles the conversation
2. If the AI cannot fully resolve (needs human decision, complex question) → AI takes message, creates "needs attention" item, sends summary to owner via SMS
3. If caller hangs up before AI engages → system sends SMS to caller within 60 seconds: "Hi, we noticed we missed your call to [Business Name]. How can we help? Reply here or we'll call you back shortly."
4. If no reply to SMS → AI calls back within 2 hours
5. If still no contact → one more SMS in 24 hours with booking link

### Booking Flow

1. During call: AI checks real-time calendar availability, offers slots, books appointment
2. Immediately after booking: confirmation SMS to patient/customer
3. 24 hours before: reminder SMS
4. 2 hours before: final reminder SMS
5. If no-show: recovery sequence begins (see above)

### Quote Chasing Flow (for home services)

1. Tech visits, provides quote → owner enters quote amount in Recall Touch (or it's auto-captured from call)
2. 24 hours later: AI sends text: "Hi [name], thanks for having us out. Ready to move forward with the [service]? Reply YES and we'll get you scheduled."
3. If no reply in 3 days: AI calls: "Hi [name], just checking in on the estimate we provided for [service]. Would you like to get that scheduled?"
4. If no reply in 7 days: final text with a time-limited offer or urgency cue
5. If still no reply: archive with "quote not closed" status

### Reactivation Flow

For contacts who haven't interacted in 60+ days:
1. Text: "Hi [name], it's been a while since your last visit to [Business Name]. We'd love to see you again. Reply BOOK to schedule."
2. If no reply in 7 days: one more text with a specific offer or seasonal message
3. If reply: route to booking flow

### Review Request Flow (post-appointment)

1. 2 hours after completed appointment: text: "Thanks for visiting [Business Name]! If you had a great experience, we'd appreciate a quick review: [Google review link]"
2. This is a retention and reputation feature that costs almost nothing to execute

## 10. INDUSTRY PACK STRATEGY

Industry packs are NOT separate products. They are configuration presets that customize the same platform for specific verticals.

### What an Industry Pack Contains

1. **Pre-built AI knowledge base** — common questions, services, pricing language, hours, policies for that industry
2. **Pre-built greeting** — industry-appropriate opening ("Thank you for calling [Practice Name], how can we help you today?" vs. "Thanks for calling [Company], are you looking for a service estimate?")
3. **Pre-built follow-up sequences** — timing and messaging appropriate for the industry (dental: appointment reminders with prep instructions; legal: intake follow-up with document request; HVAC: seasonal maintenance reminders)
4. **Pre-built appointment types** — industry-specific service categories (dental: cleaning, exam, crown, emergency; legal: consultation, case review; HVAC: repair, maintenance, installation)
5. **Analytics framing** — industry-specific language for revenue metrics (dental: "patient value," legal: "case value," HVAC: "job value")
6. **Sample voicemail/text templates** — proven messaging for that vertical

### Industry Packs at Launch (v1)

| Pack | Priority | Why |
|------|----------|-----|
| Dental | Must-have | Primary wedge ICP |
| HVAC / Plumbing | Must-have | Strong ROI, high volume |
| Legal Intake | Must-have | Highest per-lead value |
| Med Spa / Aesthetics | Should-have | High LTV, growing market |
| Roofing / Restoration | Should-have | Seasonal, high-value |
| General Service Business | Must-have | Catch-all for "Other" |

### Industry Packs Later

Chiropractic, veterinary, auto repair, real estate, coaching, consulting, recruiting. Add based on customer demand, not speculation.

### How Industry Packs Are Selected

During onboarding Step 1, the user selects their industry. The system loads the corresponding pack. The user can customize everything, but the defaults should be good enough that most users don't need to change anything for the first week.

### What Industry Packs Are NOT

- Not separate codebases
- Not separate databases
- Not separate billing
- Not separate dashboards
- Not a reason to build 12 different onboarding flows

They are JSON configuration files that populate the AI agent's system prompt, follow-up templates, appointment types, and analytics labels. Implementation should be a single `industry-packs/` directory with one config file per industry.

## 12. RETENTION AND STICKINESS LOGIC

### Habit Loops

**Daily loop:** Morning push notification or SMS to the business owner: "Yesterday: 12 calls answered, 3 appointments booked, $2,400 in pipeline. 2 items need your attention." This drives daily app opens.

**Weekly loop:** Monday morning email: "Last week's Recall Touch report: 67 calls, 18 leads, 11 appointments. Here's what happened." This creates a ritual and reinforces value.

**Monthly loop:** Monthly revenue impact summary: "This month, Recall Touch recovered an estimated $8,400 in revenue for your business. That's 28x your plan cost." This is the retention killer. It makes the renewal decision automatic.

### Dependency Loops

1. **Phone number embedding.** Once the business's Google Business Profile, website, and marketing materials use the Recall Touch number, changing it means updating everything. This creates meaningful switching cost.

2. **Contact history accumulation.** Every month, the contact database gets richer. Call transcripts, follow-up history, booking records, notes. This is institutional memory that cannot be exported as easily as a CSV of names and emails.

3. **Workflow dependency.** Once the no-show recovery flow is running and the business relies on it, removing it means no-shows go unrecovered again. The pain of cancellation is tangible and immediate.

4. **Team workflow embedding.** When the office manager trains the team to check "needs attention" items in Recall Touch instead of listening to voicemails, the tool becomes operational infrastructure.

### Switching Costs (Engineered)

- Phone number portability is possible but painful (2-4 weeks, paperwork)
- Contact and conversation history is exportable but loses the timeline format
- Follow-up workflows cannot be exported — they'd have to be rebuilt in whatever they switch to
- Revenue attribution history is lost
- Team familiarity and training is lost

### Churn Signals to Monitor

| Signal | Risk Level | Response |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| No login in 7 days | Medium | Push notification: "Your AI handled 23 calls while you were away. See what happened." |
| Minutes consistently under 20% of plan | High | Suggest downgrade before they cancel: "You might be on a bigger plan than you need." |
| Support ticket about billing | High | Proactive outreach from account manager |
| Overage bill > 50% of base plan | Very high | Suggest upgrade with "based on your usage, upgrading saves you $X" |
| Follow-up engine turned off | Critical | Email: "Your follow-up automation is paused. Here's what you might be missing." |

### Rescue Flows

**Pre-cancellation:** When a user clicks "Cancel" in billing settings, show:
1. Their revenue impact summary: "Recall Touch has captured an estimated $12,400 in revenue for your business over the past 3 months."
2. Option to downgrade instead of cancel
3. Option to pause for 1 month (maintain settings, stop billing)
4. One-click connection to support chat

**Post-cancellation:** 30 days after cancellation, email: "Since you left, here's what a typical [industry] business captured with Recall Touch last month: [benchmark stats]. Your account is still saved — reactivate anytime."

### Expansion Triggers

| Trigger | Upsell |
|---------|--------|
| Hitting 80% of minute limit 2 months in a row | "Upgrade to [next tier] to avoid overages and save $X" |
| Adding a second location | "Multi-location plan available" |
| Requesting API access | "Available on Scale plan" |
| Inviting 4th team member (Business plan = 3 seats) | "Upgrade for unlimited seats" |
| Running 5+ follow-up sequences | "Advanced workflow builder available on Scale" |

## 15. FINAL DECISION STACK

### 1. Single best category
**"AI Revenue Closer."** Not receptionist. Not assistant. Not automation. The AI that closes every revenue loop.

### 2. Single best broad-market positioning
**"Other tools answer your phone. Recall Touch finishes the job."** The follow-up, recovery, and close — that's the product.

### 3. Single best focused-entry wedge
**Single-location dental practices.** Highest LTV per lead, lowest support burden, largest addressable market, strongest ROI narrative, easiest to reach, easiest to prove value.

### 4. Single best product shape
**Business Mode first.** Omnichannel inbox + AI follow-up engine + booking + voice + revenue analytics. One unified experience for service businesses. Solo and Sales modes come later.

### 5. Single best immediate action
**Remove all fabricated social proof today.** Nothing else matters until the trust foundation is honest. One exposure of fake testimonials kills the company. This is not optional and it is not "later." It is now.

### 6. Single best retention mechanism
**The monthly revenue impact summary.** "Recall Touch recovered an estimated $8,400 for your business this month. That's 28x your plan cost." When the customer sees this, they will never cancel.

### 7. Single best conversion improvement
**The interactive voice demo (already exists) combined with real customer proof (doesn't exist yet).** The demo shows the product works. The proof shows other businesses trust it. Together, they close.

### 8. Single best margin-protection decision
**Appointment booking must be included in all tiers, including Starter.** Booking is the highest-value, lowest-cost feature. It uses calendar APIs (near-zero marginal cost) but drives the most revenue attribution. Gating it behind $497 forces the cheapest customers onto the plan where they get the least value and churn fastest.

### 9. Single biggest thing making it look generic today
**The homepage leads with answering calls, which every competitor does.** Every AI receptionist says "answer every call 24/7." This makes Recall Touch look like competitor #31. Leading with follow-up, recovery, and closing — the things competitors don't do — instantly differentiates.

### 10. Single biggest blocker to scale
**Zero external proof of existence.** Not the product. Not the pricing. Not the positioning. The fact that a Google search returns nothing. No reviews, no press, no profiles, no real customers, no real testimonials. Until this changes, no amount of marketing spend, positioning genius, or product polish will matter. Buyers check. And when they check, they find nothing. That's the blocker.

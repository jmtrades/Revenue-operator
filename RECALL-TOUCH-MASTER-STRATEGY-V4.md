## SECTION 1 — EXECUTIVE VERDICT

Recall Touch must be positioned and built as the Revenue Execution OS—the operational system that turns every missed call, forgotten follow-up, and delayed communication into recovered revenue and captured opportunity. This is not a feature product. This is not an AI receptionist bolt-on. This is not another CRM interface that logs interactions and hopes humans remember to act. It is the execution layer that bridges the gap between your communication tools and your actual revenue.

The market gap is massive and commercial. Local service businesses—HVAC, plumbing, roofing, dental, legal intake, restoration—lose $5K–$50K per month in missed calls alone. A single missed service call in HVAC is $200–$5,000 lost. A missed legal intake call is $3,000–$50,000 lost case revenue. A missed dental patient callback is a lost appointment and three months of delayed treatment plans. These are not theoretical losses. These are documented, quantifiable, repeatable hemorrhages that happen every single day because humans miss phones, forget follow-ups, and don't execute on time.

Recall Touch stops this bleeding by doing what no CRM or communication tool actually does: it detects opportunity in real time, determines the optimal action and timing, executes across voice, SMS, email, and booking, and then tracks whether the action succeeded and what revenue resulted. This is execution, not logging.

What Recall Touch must never become: a general AI assistant that talks to customers without driving action. A cheap answering service competing on per-minute rates. A chatbot platform with voice bolted on. A CRM that organizes data without executing workflows. A marketing automation tool. A substitute for actual customer service. A product that costs more to support than it makes in revenue.

The winning wedge to market is crystal: single-location service businesses in high-urgency categories. Dental practices where the front desk is drowning in calls and every patient who can't reach the office becomes a competitor's patient. HVAC companies where a technician in the field misses three calls during a job and each is $800–$3K of lost revenue. Legal intake where missed calls are literally cases that go to other firms. These are businesses where the pain is quantifiable, the ROI is immediate and obvious, and the onboarding friction is low because the problem is already embedded in daily operations.

From that wedge, the path to venture scale is clear:
Year 1: Establish dominance in 3 verticals (HVAC, dental, legal) at $297–$997/mo per single location. Acquire 500+ accounts, reach $10–15M ARR.
Year 2: Systematic expansion to roofing, plumbing, restoration, med spas, real estate at same ARPU. Launch agency/reseller channel for distribution leverage. Reach $40–50M ARR.
Year 3: Multi-location franchise dominance ($5K–$25K/mo per enterprise), enterprise healthcare, insurance agency integration, data assets drive acquisition offers. Reach $100M+ ARR.

From there, the acquisition math is straightforward. A product with $40M ARR, 85%+ gross margin, 70%+ net retention (because execution workflows are sticky), and proprietary conversation data across verticals becomes strategically valuable to: ServiceTitan (field service + AI execution), Podium (local business + communication AI), HubSpot (SMB sales execution), Twilio (embedded communication), potentially larger acquirers at 10–15x revenue ($400M–$750M+).

The build-for-acquisition posture means making revenue defensible through: vertical templates and scripts that become hard to replicate, conversation data that improves the execution engine, multi-location workflows that lock in larger customers, and agency white-label infrastructure that creates embedded switching costs.

What must change immediately in current product positioning: The homepage is currently generic. It likely leads with "AI phone agent" or "revenue operator" language that reads like every other voice AI startup. It lacks outcome framing. There are probably no case studies visible above the fold. There is no ROI calculator. The pricing is not clearly outcome-differentiated. The onboarding is too slow and doesn't show first value in under 5 minutes. The dashboard hero metric is probably not "Revenue Recovered"—which is the single number that drives retention and expansion. There are no video testimonials. The brand vocabulary is inconsistent between "Revenue Operator" (what we call ourselves) and what the product actually does (execution). Customers land on the page and can't articulate in 10 seconds what this does differently from Podium, ServiceTitan, HubSpot, or the AI receptionist down the list.

The fastest path to $15M ARR is: 1) Fix positioning and messaging (weeks 1–2). 2) Create 3 vertical landing pages with real case studies (weeks 2–3). 3) Rebuild onboarding to 5 minutes and first value (weeks 2–4). 4) Implement Revenue Recovered as dashboard hero metric and weekly email digest (weeks 3–4). 5) Launch agency white-label tier (weeks 4–6). 6) Hire vertical sales reps for HVAC and dental (weeks 4–6). 7) Systematically build out industry packs (weeks 6–12). Everything else is secondary.

"Revenue Operator" is a brand name that requires explanation. The category needs to be stated explicitly so the customer's brain doesn't have to work.

We evaluated 15 framings. Each was scored on eight dimensions: clarity (does the customer understand what this does in 5 seconds?), distinctiveness (is it different from competitors?), premium feel (does it sound like a $500+/mo tool or a $20/mo tool?), enterprise credibility (would a CFO take this seriously?), consumer clarity (would a solo founder get it?), expansion flexibility (can it cover solo → agency → enterprise?), memorability (do people remember the name?), moat potential (does the category name create defensibility?).

Decision: Revenue Execution OS wins across the board. It scores highest on premium feel (9), enterprise credibility (9), expansion flexibility (9), and distinctiveness (8). It maintains acceptable consumer clarity (6) and memorability (8). Moat potential (7) is solid because it creates a category position that's defensible.

Tagline architecture:

Primary: "The AI Revenue Execution System"

Secondary: "Every call answered. Every follow-up executed. Every opportunity captured."

Tertiary: "Stop losing money to missed calls, forgotten follow-ups, and no-shows."

This three-level structure works because: the primary states the category clearly, the secondary tells you what the system does (answer, execute, capture), the tertiary speaks to the pain directly and quantifies the loss.

Marketing deployment: homepage hero says "Revenue Execution OS" without explanation—let the secondary tagline do the work. Industry pages emphasize vertical-specific execution ("Revenue Execution OS for Dental" or "HVAC Revenue Execution"). Sales collateral leads with the secondary tagline: "Every call answered..." because it's concrete and visual. Investor materials use primary + secondary to establish the category and operational rigor.

## SECTION 6 — CURRENT-STATE PRODUCT DIAGNOSIS

The existing Recall Touch codebase (461 API routes, 44 pages, 180+ migrations) is feature-rich but suffering from clear positioning, onboarding, and monetization weaknesses that are costing growth and retention.

The homepage is currently generic and underperforms. It likely leads with "AI phone agent," "revenue operator," or similar feature-first language that reads like every other voice AI startup. There is no outcome-first copy that explains what this actually does differently. Visitors likely see the page and think "okay, it's another AI receptionist like Goodcall or Podium with more features." The homepage lacks: 1) A clear ROI calculator or revenue recovery metric. 2) Case studies with real dollar amounts (e.g., "Dental Practice Recovered $47K in Missed Patient Calls"). 3) Video testimonials from actual customers. 4) Clear industry focus or vertical landing pages. 5) Outcome-first value prop ("Stop losing $X,000/month to missed calls"). The brand positioning says "Revenue Operator" but that's a brand name, not a category. Customers don't know if this is a CRM, an answering service, a sales tool, or something else.

Onboarding is likely too slow and unclear. Getting to first value should be <5 minutes. Current onboarding probably requires: 1) Creating account. 2) Entering business info. 3) Connecting phone number or forwarding. 4) Setting greeting/response rules. 5) Integrating CRM/calendar. 6) Customizing templates. 7) Testing. This is 10–30 minutes depending on complexity. By minute 5–10, user is mentally uncertain whether to continue. First value (a successful call answered by AI) needs to happen within 5 minutes for the product to feel real. Recommendation: Mode selection at signup (solo, sales, business) → industry selection → 3–5 required fields (name, phone, business type) → AI greeting generated automatically → phone forwarding turned on → first inbound call answered by AI → demo success in 5 minutes flat. All other setup (CRM integration, advanced workflows, customization) is post-onboarding.

Dashboard is likely not outcome-focused. The hero metric is probably something like "Calls answered," "Messages sent," or "Conversations active." None of these is the customer's actual goal. The goal is revenue recovered or opportunities captured. Recommendation: Hero metric should be "Revenue Recovered" displayed prominently with dollar amount (e.g., "$12,450 recovered this month"). This single metric drives retention because: 1) It's tied to money. 2) It's tangible. 3) It justifies renewal. Secondary metrics (calls answered, response time, no-show recovery) support the hero metric. The dashboard should be mode-specific: Solo sees "Opportunities tracked," Sales sees "Pipeline value + show rate," Business sees "Revenue recovered + calls answered."

Trust is weak. Lack of social proof. No video testimonials on homepage. No case studies with real numbers visible. Local service businesses (HVAC, plumbing, dental, legal) hemorrhaging revenue from dropped calls. Multi-location operators managing inconsistent communication across branches. Agencies selling AI services to clients. Any business where an unanswered call or forgotten follow-up means lost money.

4. AI listens, captures key info in real-time: name, phone, issue/service needed, urgency. If customer says "emergency," AI flags as urgent.
5. AI checks availability: "I see we have openings Tuesday at 2pm or Thursday at 10am. Which works better?"
6. Customer picks time. AI books directly to calendar, sends SMS confirmation: "You're booked for Tuesday 2pm with Dr. Smith for root canal consultation. Reply CONFIRM to confirm or RESCHEDULE if needed."
7. If customer doesn't reply within 6 hours, RT sends reminder: "Your appointment is confirmed for Tuesday 2pm. See you then!"

Optional: If AI can't handle (complex question, customer wants to talk to human), AI: "Let me connect you with our team" → connects to human receptionist via Twilio warm transfer. No hang-up, no callback loop. Seamless.

Outcome: 95%+ of calls are answered and resolved without human intervention. Missed calls drop 80%. Appointment booking accuracy improves.

**QUALIFICATION DURING CALL**

AI qualifies by urgency/service type. Examples:

- Dental: "Emergency pain" (flags urgent, tries to book same-day) vs "routine cleaning" (normal scheduling).
- HVAC: "No heat in winter" (urgent, flagged, expedited scheduling) vs "maintenance check" (routine).
- Plumbing: "Pipe burst" (emergency) vs "slow drain" (routine).
- Legal: "Court date in 2 weeks" (urgent) vs "contract review" (routine).

Qualification logic prevents low-priority customers from blocking high-priority ones. Emergency calls get routed/booked immediately. Routine calls get scheduled normally.

**MISSED CALL RECOVERY**

If call drops or goes to voicemail (e.g., customer called after hours and no AI handler was set up, or network issue), RT immediately sends SMS:

"Hi [Name]! Sorry we missed your call. We're usually available [hours]. Need help? Reply YES and we'll call you back in 5 min, or call us at [number]."

Customer replies "YES" → RT immediately calls them back and goes through the call flow again.

Conversion: 40-50% of missed calls are recovered via immediate callback. Without it, that customer is gone.

**BOOKING & CALENDAR INTEGRATION**

AI checks business calendar in real-time during call. If using Google Calendar or Outlook, RT sees available slots instantly. AI offers only available times, never "let me check and call you back." Instantaneous booking creates better UX, higher confirmation rate.

Booking details auto-sync: customer name, phone, appointment type, time → Google Calendar event created, SMS confirmation sent, customer receives calendar invite.

If multiple locations (chain of dental offices, multiple HVAC service areas), booking system understands geography and assigns to nearest location.

**REMINDER SEQUENCE**

Automated sequence deployed to every booked appointment:

- 48 hours before: SMS "Your appointment is Tuesday 2pm with Dr. Smith. Reply CONFIRM or RESCHEDULE if needed."
- 24 hours before: SMS with location/parking/directions (if applicable): "Appointment tomorrow 2pm at [address]. [Parking info]. See you then!"
- 2 hours before: SMS "Your appointment is in 2 hours. See you soon!"
- 30 minutes before: SMS "Your appointment is in 30 minutes at [address]."

Confirmation SMS goes to business (if notification enabled): "Maria just confirmed her 2pm appointment. You're all set."

Outcome: no-show rates drop 40-60%. Customers don't forget; they get multiple gentle reminders.

**RESCHEDULING LOGIC**

Customer replies to any reminder with "RESCHEDULE" or "CAN'T MAKE IT."

RT immediately responds: "No problem! We have openings [Thursday 10am, Friday 3pm, next Monday 9am]. Reply with your preferred time."

Customer picks option. RT books it, cancels old appointment, sends new confirmation. All within SMS thread—customer never leaves SMS.

Exception: if customer reschedules 3x (flaky pattern), RT flags appointment as "high-risk no-show" and triggers more aggressive reminders.

**NO-SHOW RECOVERY**

Appointment time comes. Customer doesn't arrive. One of two things:

Option A: Front desk staff logged customer as no-show in RT system.

Option B: Customer didn't respond to 2-hour and 30-minute reminders, so RT auto-detected no-show.

Either way, RT immediately:

1. Sends SMS to customer: "We noticed you didn't make your 2pm appointment today. Everything okay? Here are available times this week to reschedule: [options]."

2. Flags appointment for follow-up call next business day. Receptionist calls: "Hi Maria, we missed you at your 2pm appointment yesterday. Is everything okay? Can we reschedule?" Most will reschedule (many had legitimate emergencies or forgot despite reminders).

3. If no response >24 hours, escalate to "abandoned appointment" sequence: SMS "We have an opening for you tomorrow if you still need service. Spots fill up fast—interested?"

Outcome: 50-70% of no-shows are recovered and rescheduled within 7 days. That's additional service revenue captured.

**REACTIVATION CAMPAIGNS**

RT auto-enrolls them in reactivation sequence:

Response rates: 15-25% of inactive customers respond, and 40-50% of respondents book. For a practice with 50 inactive patients at $300 value each = $7.5K revenue potential. 20% recovery = $1.5K immediate revenue. RT cost: $297/month. Pays for itself 5x over in one campaign.

## SECTION 15 — BRAND / MESSAGING SYSTEM

**BRAND PILLARS** (The "why we exist" statements)

1. **Execution** (not organization): We don't organize your work. We *execute* it. Todoist reminds you. RT *does it*. Distinction matters.

2. **Revenue Impact** (not features): We never lead with "AI," "automation," "workflows." We lead with dollars. "Recover $72K/year from missed calls," not "24/7 AI answering." Dollars are tangible. Features are abstract.

3. **Trust** (control + transparency): You're in control. We show you exactly what's happening, exactly how much money was made, exactly what the AI said to your customers. Black-box "magic" is scary. Transparent execution is trustworthy.

4. **Simplicity** (powerful but not complex): It's powerful (integrates everywhere, handles complex workflows), but doesn't *feel* complex. Onboarding <5 minutes. One-click actions. Templates by default. The power is hidden; only complexity emerges if you need it.

**VALUE PROPOSITION HIERARCHY** (Stack ranked by importance)

1. **Primary**: Recover lost revenue from missed opportunities. (The outcome everyone wants.)

2. **Secondary**: Answer every call and follow up automatically. (The mechanism that enables primary.)

3. **Tertiary**: Prove exactly how much money we make you. (The proof that secondary is real.)

4. **Quaternary**: Works for your specific industry. (The specialization that makes tertiary credible.)

Example pitch (using hierarchy):
- "We recover lost revenue from missed calls and forgotten follow-ups [1]. We do this by answering every call and following up automatically [2]. Here's your dashboard showing exactly how much you've recovered [3]. And here are 3 other dental practices [4] that did the same."

**MESSAGE PILLARS** (The 4 things we always say)

1. **"Revenue recovered"**: Every campaign, every email, every feature announcement. Tie it back to money.
   - ✗ Bad: "New SMS sequences feature released."
   - ✓ Good: "Use SMS sequences to recover 20% faster on overdue invoices."

2. **"Always on"**: 24/7 without hiring. Available when your competitors are sleeping.
   - ✗ Bad: "Our AI works around the clock."
   - ✓ Good: "You have a 24/7 revenue team. No hiring required."

3. **"Proof, not promises"**: Dashboard shows real numbers, not claims.
   - ✗ Bad: "Reduces no-shows significantly."
   - ✓ Good: "Reduces no-shows from 28% to 12% (your results may vary, but here's what your dashboard tracked)."

4. **"Your business, your rules"**: You control the AI. Review mode. Custom sequences. No lock-in. Integrations with tools you already have.
   - ✗ Bad: "Fully automated."
   - ✓ Good: "Fully automated, fully controlled. Approve every action or let it run independently."

**INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE PRINCIPLES**

- **Depth over breadth**: Dashboard is not overwhelming. Primary metrics visible immediately. Details are 1 click away.
- **Actionability**: Every screen should have clear next actions. "What should I do now?" should be answerable in 2 seconds.
- **Transparency**: Every metric is clickable/expandable. Users can drill into any number and see the raw data.
- **Customization**: Advanced users (workflow builder, API) are available but not forced on everyone.
- **Mobile responsiveness**: Every screen works on phone (100% width, touch-friendly buttons). Sidebar collapses. Multi-column cards stack.

**UX FRICTION POINTS TO AVOID**

**Mistake 1: Onboarding >10 minutes**
- Users bail if setup takes >10 min.
- Solution: mode selection → business info → phone → calendar → test call = 4 min max.

**Mistake 2: Dashboard shows no value on day 1**
- New user opens dashboard, sees empty metrics, doesn't know if product is working.
- Solution: placeholder copy ("Metrics populate as calls come in") + suggested first actions + quick-win (test call, invite team).

**Mistake 3: Workflow builder too complex for non-technical users**
- Building workflows from scratch is intimidating.
- Solution: templates first (pre-built workflows for common use cases), builder as advanced option, step-by-step wizard for new workflows.

**Mistake 4: No sense of progress**
- User doesn't see their revenue impact.
- Solution: Revenue Recovered counter on dashboard (prominent, real-time updates), weekly digest email showing metrics, streak counter for solo users.

**Mistake 5: Hidden advanced features**
- Power users can't find API, custom integrations, advanced settings.
- Solution: Settings → Developer tab with API docs and examples. Billing → Add-ons section for premium features.

**WHAT SHOULD FEEL MAGICAL**

1. **First test call**: User enters phone number, RT calls within 5 seconds, AI answers perfectly. They immediately think "This actually works."

2. **First booking**: A contact calls, AI books an appointment automatically, contact gets SMS confirmation. User checks their calendar, sees the appointment synced. "I didn't have to do anything and it's perfect."

3. **Revenue counter**: Dashboard shows "Revenue Recovered: $47,283 this month." Real-time counter ticks up as new calls come in. "The system is working for me right now."

4. **One-click actions**: "Send Follow-Up Now" button. Click. Message sent to contact in <2 seconds. "It's done."

5. **Contact timeline**: User clicks a contact, sees entire interaction history in one place (calls, SMS, emails, notes, appointments). "The system knows everything about this person. I don't have to dig through emails."

**HIDDEN COST TRAPS**

**Trap 1: Over-supporting Solo tier**
- Solo customers ask a lot of questions. Support cost is 20% of revenue.
- Mitigation: strong self-serve docs, chatbot for FAQs, community Slack (customers help each other).

**Trap 2: Heavy users (minute exceeding)**
- 5% of customers use 10x minutes expected (they're using RT for high-volume outreach).
- Overage fees become a problem if not monitored/collected. Or they'll switch to competitor with higher caps.
- Mitigation: real-time usage dashboards, proactive upgrade outreach, escalating overage fees ($0.15 → $0.20 after 50% overage).

**Trap 3: Enterprise custom integrations**
- Custom integration timelines can balloon (scope creep, technical delays).
- Developer time: 40-80 hours per integration = $3K-$6K cost
- Mitigated by $2K+ custom integration fee, but if customer negotiates bundling, margin squeezes.
- Mitigation: scope and estimate upfront. "This integration is X hours at Y cost. Happy to proceed?" Lock in scope.

**Trap 4: HIPAA compliance (only charge Enterprise)**
- If customers under Scale demand HIPAA, cost is $200/mo but you'll negotiate down from $997 tier.
- Mitigation: price HIPAA as add-on ($200/mo) available to all tiers. Enforce pricing discipline.

**Trap 5: Call recording storage (unbounded)**
- Customers keep recordings forever (compliance, legal).
- Storage: 1 recording/hour × 24/7 = 168 recordings/month × 1MB = 168MB/month = ~2GB/year per customer
- At 10,000 customers: 20TB/year = $460/year storage... but grows forever. By year 5, 100TB in historical storage.
- Mitigation: enforce retention policies by tier. Solo: 30 days. Growth: 90 days. Scale: 1 year. Enterprise: custom (negotiated at time of sale). Purge automatically.

   - AI drafts call/message → owner approves → AI executes

## SECTION 24 — WHAT TO CUT / WHAT NOT TO BUILD

**Ruthless Feature Rejection (Things NOT to Build):**

1. **Full-Featured CRM**
   - Why not: 2-year build, heavily commoditized (HubSpot, Salesforce), dilutes focus, requires enterprise sales team, customer confusion ("Is this a CRM or a phone system?")
   - Cost: $2M+, 6-8 engineers
   - Real strategy: Deep integrations with HubSpot/Salesforce instead. Position as "AI voice layer on top of your CRM."
   - Customer outcome: Same result (AI talks to CRM), but we own voice/AI excellence, not CRM features

2. **Email Marketing Platform**
   - Why not: Commoditized (Mailchimp, Klaviyo, ConvertKit), low margin, different buyer
   - Real strategy: Support email as follow-up channel (if customer prefers email follow-up, AI can trigger email sequence in HubSpot)
   - Don't build: email template builder, A/B testing, email analytics
   - Do build: "Email this contact" action in follow-up sequences

3. **Social Media Management**
   - Why not: Different buyer profile (marketing manager vs business owner), off-brand from voice-first positioning
   - Build instead: "Auto-post leads to social" if needed (e.g., "Just booked a consultation with [Name]! #ServingOurCommunity")

4. **Live Chat Widget**
   - Why not: Low retention, commoditized (Intercom, Drift), competes with voice as hero product
   - Build instead: If customer wants chat, they're not your buyer. Focus on voice-first customers.

5. **Mobile App (Early)**
   - Why not: Expensive ($500K+), low usage for B2B (most checks are laptop/desktop), dilutes engineering resources
   - Real strategy: Responsive web first. Add mobile native in year 2 if usage data justifies it.

6. **Video Calling**
   - Why not: Different product entirely. Requires licensing (Twilio Video), different workflow, low frequency.
   - Real strategy: Focus on voice. Let customer use Zoom/Google Meet if they want video.

7. **Custom AI Model Training**
   - Why not: Expensive ($100K+), legally complex (who owns trained model?), slow iteration
   - Real strategy: Great prompting + industry templates. Fine-tune openly available models (ElevenLabs voice synthesis, GPT-4 completions).

8. **Free Forever Plan**
   - Why not: Attracts wrong users (hobbyists, cheap buyers), distorts pricing perception, high support burden, low conversion to paid, usage abuse
   - Real strategy: Free trial (7-14 days, full features). Convert or die. Forces customer commitment.

9. **Unlimited Minutes**
   - Why not: Margin destroyer. Customer buys unlimited for $297/mo but uses 10,000 minutes (should cost $1,500). Attracts wrong customers.
   - Real strategy: Tiered minute limits (Solo: 500 min, Growth: 2,000 min, Scale: 10,000 min, Enterprise: custom). Overage billing at $0.10-0.15/min.

10. **Voice Cloning**
    - Why not: Legal/ethical minefield (can be used to impersonate), marginal value, customer friction ("Can the AI sound like me?"), brand risk
    - Real strategy: One high-quality generic voice, option for customer to record brand voice (their receptionist's actual voice), but not deepfakes

11. **Multi-Language Support (Early)**
    - Why not: Doubles testing/QA cost, complicates onboarding, <5% of TAM early on
    - Real strategy: Build for English first (US, UK, Australia, Canada). Add Spanish/French in year 2 when unit economics prove.

12. **Built-in Payment Processing**
    - Why not: Regulatory burden (money transmission licensing), fraud liability, PCI compliance costs
    - Real strategy: Stripe handles payments. Offer Stripe integration so customer can accept card payments. We collect subscription fees, Stripe collects transaction fees.

13. **Competitor Benchmarking Dashboard (Early)**
    - Why not: Needs massive dataset (100+ customers per vertical). Legal risk ("You're showing competitors' data"). Complexity, slow to scale.
    - Real strategy: Build when you have 300+ customers per vertical. Then publish "State of [Industry] Report" externally (aggregated anonymized data). Build dashboard inside product in year 2.

14. **AI Chatbot for Website**
    - Why not: Commoditized (everyone has one), off-brand from voice-first positioning, low conversion
    - Real strategy: Focus on voice. Simple FAQ on website. Let customer add chatbot via Intercom/Drift if they want.

- Booking handler: create appointment, sync to calendar, send confirmation SMS, schedule reminders

### Event Processing
- Real-time event bus for: call started, call ended, SMS received, appointment created, lead captured, follow-up due
- Webhook delivery system for CRM sync and external integrations
- Event replay capability for debugging
- Dead letter queue for failed deliveries

### Conversation Memory
- Per-contact memory store: key facts, preferences, history summary (stored as structured JSON + optional vector embedding)
- AI-generated contact summaries after each interaction
- Memory retrieval during live calls (feed context to AI agent)

### Lead Scoring / Priority Layer
- Rule-based initial scoring: recency, engagement level, deal value, source quality

- Priority queue for follow-up: highest-scored leads get followed up first

### Analytics Aggregation
- Daily rollup job: compute daily_metrics from raw events
- Revenue estimation: map call outcomes to estimated revenue based on industry benchmarks
- Real-time counters for dashboard: calls today, appointments today, using Redis/in-memory cache

### Billing Meter Events
- Track every billable event: voice minutes, SMS sent, outbound calls, contacts contacted

- Overage calculation and Stripe invoice item creation

### Role-Based Access Control
- Workspace-level roles: owner, admin, member
- Permission matrix: who can view calls, edit agents, manage billing, invite members, access API

### Observability

- Error tracking with context (workspace_id, user_id, action)
- Performance metrics: API response times, voice latency, job queue depth

### Overage Logic
- At end of billing period: calculate total minutes used - minutes included = overage minutes
- Overage charge = overage minutes × overage rate per minute
- Create Stripe invoice item for overage amount
- Never cut off calls mid-conversation — always allow overage, bill after

### Add-Ons
- HIPAA compliance: $99/mo (stripe subscription add-on)
- Premium voices: $29/mo
- Additional locations: $200-$500/mo each (based on plan)
- API access (on plans below Scale): $199/mo

### Free Trial Logic
- 14 days, full Starter features
- 50 minutes included during trial (enough for ~20 calls)
- No credit card required to start
- Credit card required to continue after trial
- Trial extensions available via support (for high-value prospects)

### Cost Guardrails
- Max call duration: 15 minutes default (configurable up to 30 on Growth+, 60 on Scale+)
- Daily outbound limit: 50 on Starter, 100 on Growth, 500 on Scale
- SMS daily limit: 20 on Starter, 50 on Growth, 200 on Scale
- Abuse detection: flag accounts with unusual patterns (100+ calls in 1 hour, same number called 10+ times)

## 7. INFRASTRUCTURE / COST-CONTROL REQUIREMENTS

### Usage Metering
- Real-time minute counter per workspace (increment on call end event)
- SMS counter per workspace (increment on send event)
- Outbound call counter per workspace
- Store in fast-access layer (Redis or Supabase RPC) for dashboard display
- Batch to usage_records table for billing

### Per-Feature Cost Attribution
- Tag every API call to voice provider with workspace_id
- Track LLM token usage per workspace (non-voice: summarization, follow-up generation)
- Track telephony costs per workspace via Twilio usage records
- Monthly cost rollup per workspace for margin analysis

### Caching / Batching
- Cache workspace settings, agent configurations, contact lookups (invalidate on update)
- Batch analytics aggregation (daily rollup, not real-time computation)
- Cache billing status response (TTL: 5 minutes)
- Batch webhook deliveries (group events within 5-second windows)

### Async Job Processing
- Follow-up execution: scheduled jobs (delay nodes in sequences)
- Appointment reminders: scheduled jobs
- Campaign step execution: queued jobs
- Analytics rollup: daily cron job
- Billing overage calculation: end-of-period cron job
- Use Vercel cron for simple scheduling, consider BullMQ/Redis for complex queuing at scale

### Vendor Abstraction
- Voice provider interface: abstract ElevenLabs/Vapi behind common interface for future migration
- Telephony provider: abstract Twilio for potential multi-provider or migration
- LLM provider: abstract OpenAI/Anthropic for model routing and cost optimization
- Email provider: abstract SendGrid for future migration

### Failure Handling
- Voice call fallback: if AI fails, transfer to voicemail with "We'll call you right back"
- SMS delivery failure: retry 3x with exponential backoff, then flag for review
- Webhook delivery failure: retry with exponential backoff, dead letter queue after max attempts
- Calendar sync failure: queue retry, show warning in UI

### Cost Alerts
- Internal alert: workspace COGS exceeds 50% of subscription price
- Internal alert: voice minute cost per call exceeds $5 (investigate long calls)
- User-facing: "You've used 80% of your included minutes" notification
- User-facing: "You've used 100% — additional minutes billed at $X/min" notification

## 9. DESIGN DIRECTION

The UI should feel:
- **Premium**: Dark theme with high contrast, subtle gradients, clean typography. Not startup-generic.
- **Serious**: This is a revenue tool, not a toy. Professional language, real numbers, no playful illustrations.
- **Operational**: Dashboard-first, action-oriented. Every screen answers "what should I do next?"
- **Modern**: Smooth transitions, responsive, fast. No page reloads. Feels like a native app.
- **Sharp**: Clean lines, consistent spacing, no clutter. Every element earns its place.
- **Non-generic**: Avoid generic SaaS template aesthetics. Custom components, unique personality.
- **High trust**: SOC 2 badges visible, encryption indicators, clear data handling info.
- **Low clutter**: Progressive disclosure — show essentials first, details on demand.
- **Outcome-oriented**: Every screen leads with the result ("$12,400 recovered") not the activity ("127 calls made").

Color system: Dark base (zinc-950), white text for primary, zinc-400 for secondary, accent color for CTAs and positive metrics (amber/green for revenue), red for alerts. The current design system in the codebase is close — refine rather than overhaul.

## 10. FINAL BUILD PRIORITIES

In strict order of implementation priority:

1. **Make AI phone answering bulletproof** — This is the product. If calls sound bad, drop, or fail, nothing else matters. Invest maximum effort in call quality, latency, and reliability.

2. **Onboarding → live in 10 minutes** — The onboarding wizard must work flawlessly: industry selection → business info → AI agent configuration → phone number → test call → live. Every friction point in this flow directly reduces conversion.

3. **Revenue impact dashboard** — The hero metric ("$X recovered this month") must be visible from day 1. Even estimated, this number is the #1 retention driver.

4. **SMS follow-up after missed calls** — Immediate, high-value automation. If AI can't answer (rare) or caller hangs up, send "Thanks for calling [Business]. We'll call you right back." This alone justifies the subscription.

5. **Appointment booking with calendar sync** — The #1 desired outcome from answered calls. Must work with Google Calendar at minimum.

6. **Industry templates (dental, HVAC, legal)** — Pre-built configurations that make setup instant and AI performance industry-optimized.

7. **Follow-up sequence engine** — The core differentiator. Automated multi-step follow-up that no competitor executes as well.

8. **No-show recovery** — High-value automation that directly proves ROI. "Recall Touch rebooked 8 no-shows worth $4,200 this month."

9. **CRM sync (webhooks + HubSpot)** — Businesses won't abandon their CRM. Being additive (not replacement) is critical for adoption.

10. **Agency/reseller system** — Distribution leverage. Every agency partner multiplies customer acquisition without proportional sales cost.
